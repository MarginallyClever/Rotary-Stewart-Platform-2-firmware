The switches

Soldering

red is the pin closest to the roller.

black is the center pin.

blue is the pin closest to the clicker.

Power supply

move the jumper on the RUMBA from "Stand alone" to "USB power".

Upload & Run your first Arduino Test program

connect the RUMBA controller to your computer with the USB cable provided.

connect the switch to an input.

Start Arduino software.

Change Arduino > Tools > Board to "Arduino Mega 2560 or Mega ADK".

Change Arduino > Tools > Serial Port.

  • Windows: Probably the last COM port listed.
  • OSX/linux: Something similar to /dev/tty.usbmodemfa131.

In Arduino software open test_switches.ino

In Arduino software click the "upload" button.

In Arduino software open the Serial Monitor window (magnifying glass icon on the top right). You should see garbage characters in the window.

In the Serial Monitor window set the baud rate to 57600 (bottom right corner). You should now see six columns of '1's.

Press the switch. A single column should change from 1 to 0. If it doesn't, recheck your connections between the switch and the RUMBA.

Close the serial window.

Go out the way you came in
Make this a habit! If you disconnect USB while the serial window is open in Windows you will have to reboot your PC before windows can "find" the COM port again.

Disconnect USB.

Install

Thread the wire for two switches through the hole in each face. secure the switches with zip ties so that the roller is downward.

Switches 1 and 2 should be side-by-side with switch 1 on the right.

Switches 3 and 4 should be side-by-side with switch 3 on the right.

Switches 5 and 6 should be side-by-side with switch 5 on the right.

Test 2

Connect the RUMBA controller to your computer with the USB cable provided.

Test the switches again to be sure they are in the correct order.

Disconnect USB.
